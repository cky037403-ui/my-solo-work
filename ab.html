📜 The Historical Journey

A Crowd-Funded Vision: Unlike many national monuments, it wasn’t funded by the government. In the 1920s, the Catholic Circle of Rio organized "Monument Week" to collect donations from everyday Brazilians to build a symbol that would reclaim the city for faith. The Design Shift: The original sketch featured Jesus holding a large cross in one hand and a celestial globe in the other. It was later simplified to the Art Deco "open arms" design, transforming the statue itself into a giant cross. Construction (1922–1931): It took nine years to complete. The head and hands were sculpted in France by Paul Landowski, then shipped to Brazil in pieces to be carried up the mountain by train.
