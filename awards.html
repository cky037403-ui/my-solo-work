EDINBURGH CASTLE

Perched atop the volcanic Castle Rock, Edinburgh Castle is Scotland’s most iconic historic fortress and a dominant feature of the city's skyline. For centuries, it served as a royal residence, military stronghold, and prison, witnessing pivotal moments in Scottish history from the Wars of Independence to the Jacobite Risings. Today, it houses the Honours of Scotland (the oldest Crown Jewels in Britain) and the 12th-century St Margaret’s Chapel, the city's oldest surviving building. Visitors flock there to experience the daily One O’Clock Gun tradition and enjoy unparalleled panoramic views of the capital.
