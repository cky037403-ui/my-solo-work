RUINE OF ST.PAUL

The Ruins of St. Paul’s are the 17th-century remains of the Church of Mater Dei, once part of Asia’s first Western-style university before a 1835 fire destroyed everything but its facade. This "sermon in stone" is famous for blending Eastern and Western symbols, featuring Japanese chrysanthemums and Chinese inscriptions alongside Catholic saints. Today, the granite wall stands as Macau's most iconic landmark, hiding a crypt behind it that houses the remains of Japanese and Vietnamese martyrs.
